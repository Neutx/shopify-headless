General shape profile comparison
Both the X2H CrazyLight and X2 feature an ergonomically symmetrical design
when viewed from above, providing a comfortable fit and excellent control.
Compared to the X2, the X2H CrazyLight has a higher back design that offers
more support for the palm, resulting in a more comfortable grip—especially for
claw and relaxed claw grip styles.
Hump Comparison
The X2 features a gently curved hump that gradually rises from the front to the
center, peaking in the middle and tapering smoothly toward the rear. This design
creates a soft and comfortable click area, naturally guiding the ring and pinky
fingers downward for a stable and intuitive grip. In contrast, the X2H CrazyLight
has a higher central hump and a steeper rear slope compared to the X2,
allowing the hand to rest more naturally and providing a more stable and
comfortable grip.
Symmetrical Click Heights
Both the X2 and X2H CrazyLight feature a symmetrical click height design, with
buttons placed at the same level. While the X2 has a lower and wider button
layout, the X2H CrazyLight is designed with a slightly narrower structure,
optimized for claw and relaxed claw grips, providing ergonomic comfort and
enhanced grip during use.
